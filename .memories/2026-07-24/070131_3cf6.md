## Prepared the v0.1.0-rc.4 sidecar candidate

### WHAT

Combined the RC3 Apple proof harness with the `apple-x64-metal-portable` package lane and advanced the local candidate to `0.1.0-rc.4`. Added truthful RC4 release notes, current-candidate documentation, structural release/workflow contracts, and a complete phase report.

### WHY

Miller needs a shared accelerated semantic sidecar candidate that can package Apple arm64 and x64 Metal without converting hosted compile/package evidence into unsupported physical-hardware claims. The proof harness also needed stronger failure semantics before it could be relied on for promotion evidence.

### HOW

- Kept Apple x64 as a package candidate pending exact-archive proof on a physical Intel Mac.
- Hardened throughput and concurrency harnesses with backend binding, minimum gate shape, ordered protocol validation, bounded response waits, machine-readable exit-2 errors, and child cleanup.
- Brought Bash and PowerShell hardware-smoke adapters into parity for backend binding, Python discovery, conformance identity, and timeout cleanup.
- Preserved RC3 evidence as historical checksum- and harness-hash-bound proof that does not transfer to RC4.
- Built the Apple arm64 RC4 archive twice and verified byte identity plus checksum-bound unpacked artifact validation.
- Repeated fresh Claude reviews until the final result was `approve` with `findings: []`.

### IMPACT

The branch is locally ready to commit as the RC4 source/package candidate. It adds the hosted macOS x64 package lane while keeping support claims honest and makes promotion evidence fail closed instead of hanging, crashing ambiguously, or accepting malformed protocol data.
